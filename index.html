Skopos Theory

According to Hans Vermeer, translation is mainly an intention-based work. In memes, "fidelity to the original" is secondary in relation to "fidelity to the purpose."

▼ Read more

Priority of pragmatics. If the goal of a meme is to make a local community laugh, complete deconstruction of the source text is permitted. Literal translation often leads to "humor entropy," where the meaning is clear but the comic effect is zero.

Flexibility of rules. It's okay to redo the tone, swap characters or cultural realities if this helps to achieve the desired communicative effect on the target audience.

Situation analysis. Before starting work, it is important to determine whether the meme is informative (the context needs to be explained) or purely attractive (it just needs to make people laugh).

📊 In our survey from 50 participants 78% find the memified format of academic instructions motivating.
🎭

Multimodality

An approach in which the translator works not only with text, but with all other channels of meaning transmission (modalities), such as images, sound, video, etc. A meme is not just text with a picture, but a single semiotic object. The translation must take into account the "visual anchors" that already convey meaning.

▼ Read more

Third meaning. It is important to realize that text and images may mean one thing separately, but together they create a "third meaning." The translator should work not with the phrase, but with this new entity.

Visual Anchoring. If a character in the image has a specific expression, the text must explain the reason for this particular physiological reaction. You cannot write text that contradicts the character's micro-expressions.

Spatial limitation. The visual format dictates conciseness. The translation should not clutter the frame, otherwise the "weight" of the text will kill the ease of perception.

Translation strategies

When translating memes, it is possible to use completely different strategies. We deliberately exaggerated in the examples above and would like to point out that the best final option may be a combination of several strategies, or something new, depending on the goal set for the translator (Skopos).
